## Executive Summary: Project Weekly Status — At Risk

The project is currently flagged as **At Risk**, driven by two unresolved medium-severity issues that together point to a broader pattern of communication fragmentation and undocumented decision-making. The most immediate concern involves a stakeholder communication breakdown stemming from a 14-day delayed response by the Account Manager regarding client feedback on the homepage design. While the client has approved the overall design, a requested header modification was communicated outside of the main project thread via a separate email, leaving the broader team without visibility into the specific change required. Although the Project Manager has acknowledged the gap and directed that the item be added to the backlog, the modification details have yet to be formally shared with the team, creating a risk that development work may proceed without full alignment on the client's expectations.

Compounding this communication risk is an emerging scope and governance concern related to product filtering logic. A business rule question — specifically, how to surface products with only partially available variants under an "in stock" filter — was raised by the Business Analyst and answered directionally by the Project Manager without client validation or formal documentation. While the PM's proposed approach is reasonable, an undocumented, unilaterally made business logic decision of this nature carries meaningful risk of misalignment with client expectations and potential rework downstream. Taken together, both flags underscore the need for tighter communication protocols, centralized documentation of client-facing decisions, and a formal confirmation loop with the client before ambiguous requirements are built upon.
