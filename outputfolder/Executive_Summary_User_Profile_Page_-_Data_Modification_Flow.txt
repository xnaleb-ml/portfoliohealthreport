## Executive Summary: User Profile Page – Data Modification Flow

The User Profile Page – Data Modification Flow project is currently assessed as **At Risk**, with two unresolved medium-severity flags identified as of early July 2025. The first concern involves a potential scope creep situation stemming from an undocumented feature request: the addition of a post-password-change confirmation email. While the security rationale for this enhancement is sound and has garnered developer support, it was introduced outside the formal specification process and has yet to receive proper scope approval, effort estimation, or sprint assignment. Without these governance steps, the team risks absorbing unplanned work that could affect delivery timelines and resource allocation without visibility at the leadership level.

The second flag highlights a resource and dependency bottleneck related to outstanding client-side creative assets. The test environment remains populated with placeholder imagery due to the non-delivery of homepage banner assets from the client, limiting the team's ability to validate the product against real content. Although a responsible party has been identified and a follow-up was committed to, no confirmed delivery timeline has been established, leaving this dependency unresolved and potentially impeding testing progress. Taken together, these two issues reflect a need for tighter scope governance and more proactive client dependency management. It is recommended that the Project Manager prioritize formal resolution of both items in the immediate term to prevent further schedule or quality risk.
