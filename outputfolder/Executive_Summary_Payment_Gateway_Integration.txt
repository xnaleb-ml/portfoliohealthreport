## Executive Summary: Payment Gateway Integration — Project Health Alert

The Payment Gateway Integration project is currently assessed as **At Risk**, with two notable flags identified as of June 19, 2025. The most pressing concern is an unresolved and unowned question regarding API rate-limiting behavior. Raised by the Business Analyst during an error handling discussion, the question of how the system should respond when request thresholds to the payment gateway are exceeded has received no acknowledgment, no assigned owner, and no defined resolution path. This gap represents a meaningful emerging risk: without a deliberate strategy for rate-limiting, the integration could be vulnerable to cascading failures or degraded performance under real-world load conditions, and the longer this remains unaddressed, the greater the likelihood it surfaces as a critical defect in later stages of development or in production.

A secondary concern involves an informal, undocumented expansion of project scope related to error handling logic for TIMEOUT and INVALID_CARD gateway responses. While this item has been functionally resolved — the Business Analyst provided verbal guidance and the Project Manager offered informal approval — the underlying issue is that this logic was absent from the original specification and has not been formally documented or incorporated into the requirements baseline. Although the immediate development path is now clear, this pattern of scope being defined and approved outside of formal channels introduces risk to change control integrity, audit readiness, and downstream testing coverage. It is recommended that the team formalize the rate-limiting question as an urgent action item with a named owner and target resolution date, while also updating the specification to reflect the newly defined error handling behaviors before development progresses further.
