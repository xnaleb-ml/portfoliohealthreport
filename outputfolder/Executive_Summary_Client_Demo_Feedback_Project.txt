# Executive Summary: Client Demo Feedback Project

Following the client demo, the **Client Demo Feedback Project** has been flagged as **At Risk**, driven by an emerging scope creep concern that requires prompt leadership attention. The client introduced four post-demo UI/UX change requests — slider image optimization, Contact button resizing, footer logo hyperlinking, and login button repositioning — none of which were part of the originally agreed project scope. While the Frontend Developer and Senior Developer have acknowledged three of these items and provided preliminary estimates, the introduction of these requests without a formal change control process represents a risk to timeline, resource allocation, and scope boundaries. Left unmanaged, this pattern of informal scope expansion could set a precedent for additional unvetted requests as the project progresses.

Of particular concern is the footer logo hyperlinking request, which remains entirely unacknowledged, unassigned, and unestimated by any team member as of June 9, 2025. This gap indicates a breakdown in action item ownership and follow-through, compounded by the flag currently sitting unassigned at the team level. It is recommended that leadership intervene to formally triage all four change requests through the appropriate scope management process, assign clear ownership for the outstanding footer logo item, and establish explicit communication with the client regarding what will and will not be accommodated within the current engagement. Proactive resolution of these items now will prevent downstream delivery risk and protect the integrity of the project's original commitments.
