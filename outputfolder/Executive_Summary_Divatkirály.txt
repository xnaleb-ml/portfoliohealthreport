## Executive Summary – Project DIVATKIRÁLY | Status: AT RISK

Project DIVATKIRÁLY is currently carrying three unresolved attention flags, two of which are rated high severity, and the combined effect represents a meaningful threat to both delivery timeline and legal compliance. The most operationally critical issue involves the Barion V2 payment integration, where a 14-day unanswered technical ambiguity around API callback handling caused a full development stall. Although the Business Analyst eventually provided directional guidance on June 24th, neither a formal specification update nor verified testing progress has been documented as of the latest available data. Compounding this concern, the Project Manager's own status check on the same date revealed no awareness of the open blocker, pointing to a systemic gap in issue visibility and escalation monitoring that warrants immediate process correction. Separately, a GDPR compliance violation — the newsletter subscription checkbox being pre-checked by default during registration — was flagged by the client and assigned for remediation, but the responsible developer initially misunderstood the scope of the fix. Corrective instruction has been issued, however no implementation confirmation has been received, leaving a legally non-compliant feature in an unverified state.

Beyond these two high-severity items, a medium-severity scope creep risk has emerged following the belated surfacing of a client request to extend search functionality to include SKU and item number lookups, a capability not covered in the current specification. While the backend developer has acknowledged the work and estimated approximately half a day of effort, the thread closed without confirmation that a JIRA ticket was created, that the client was formally notified of the out-of-scope classification, or that any budget or timeline impact was explicitly accepted by the client. Taken together, these three flags paint a picture of a project under pressure on multiple fronts simultaneously — technical, legal, and commercial — and immediate leadership attention is recommended to close each item with documented, confirmed resolution rather than directional guidance alone.
